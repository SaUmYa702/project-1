Some Question:
What is the main purpose of analyzing the Kissan Call Centre data?

The primary purpose of analyzing the Kissan Call Centre data is to gain insights into the agricultural challenges and needs of farmers. By examining the types of inquiries and concerns raised by farmers, we can identify common issues, regional differences, and emerging agricultural trends.

This helps in improving the services provided by the call center, enabling targeted support, and aiding policymakers in making informed decisions to support the farming community.

What are the key data points collected by the Kissan Call Centre?

Farmer's region and location: Helps identify regional agricultural needs. Nature of inquiry: Covers topics like crop management, pest control, weather conditions, and market prices.

Call duration and time: Provides insights into how long farmers typically need assistance and peak call times. Follow-up actions: Tracks if additional help or follow-up is needed after the initial call. These data points help create a comprehensive understanding of the agricultural ecosystem.

How can data visualization help in understanding the Kissan Call Centre data?

Data visualization plays a critical role in simplifying complex data and making insights more accessible. By creating charts, graphs, and maps, we can:

Identify trends and patterns in agricultural issues, such as the most common inquiries or regional challenges. Visualize the frequency and distribution of calls, allowing for better resource allocation and service improvements. Communicate insights effectively to stakeholders, including government bodies, agricultural experts, and farmers themselves.

Objectives:

1.Analyze Farmer Inquiries:



To analyze the types of questions and concerns raised by farmers, identify the most common agricultural issues, and understand the regional variations in these concerns.

2.Identify Key Agricultural Trends:



To uncover patterns related to crop management, pest control, market prices, weather-related challenges, and other agricultural practices through the data collected by the Kissan Call Centre.

3. Visualize Data for Better Understanding:



To create clear and effective visualizations (graphs, charts, maps) that represent the collected data, making it easier to understand and interpret for various stakeholders such as policymakers, agricultural experts, and the farming community.
